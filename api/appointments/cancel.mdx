## Authorization

This endpoint requires authentication with any of the following authorities:
- `PACIENTE` - Can cancel their own appointments
- `MEDICO` - Can cancel any appointment
- `ADMIN` - Can cancel any appointment

<ParamField path="id" type="integer" required>
  The unique identifier of the appointment to cancel
</ParamField>

## Behavior

### For Patients

When a patient cancels an appointment:
- The patient can only cancel their own appointments
- The appointment status returns to `DISPONIBLE`
- The `pacienteId` field is cleared (set to null)
- The `motivo` field is cleared (set to null)
- The appointment becomes available for other patients to book

### For Doctors and Admins

When a doctor or admin cancels an appointment:
- They can cancel any appointment in the system
- The same status change occurs (returns to `DISPONIBLE`)
- Patient information is cleared from the appointment

## Notes

- Cancelling preserves the appointment slot in the system (unlike [delete](/api/appointments/delete))
- The appointment automatically becomes available for other patients
- The time slot is freed up and will appear in the [available appointments](/api/appointments/available) list
- Patients can only cancel confirmed appointments that belong to them
- Business rules regarding cancellation policies are enforced at the service layer

<Info>
  **Cancel vs Delete**: Use cancel when you want to free up a time slot for other patients. Use [delete](/api/appointments/delete) only when you want to permanently remove the appointment slot from the system.
</Info>

## Authorization Logic

The service layer validates that:
- Patients can only cancel appointments where they are the assigned patient
- Doctors and admins can cancel any appointment
- The appointment must be in a cancellable state
